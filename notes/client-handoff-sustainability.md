# Client Handoff and Sustainability

## Purpose

A research archive is only successful if it can continue to grow after the first launch. Client handoff and sustainability planning help make the site understandable, maintainable, and expandable for non-technical or semi-technical users.

## Handoff Goals

- Give the client ownership of the archive structure.
- Explain how to add or edit records.
- Clarify how documents and metadata should be managed.
- Reduce dependency on the original builder for routine updates.
- Preserve consistency as the dataset expands.
- Document known limitations and future improvements.

## What To Document

- How the archive is organized.
- What content types or record categories exist.
- How metadata fields should be used.
- How to add new documents or resources.
- How navigation and pages are structured.
- How to check public-facing pages after updates.
- What changes should be handled carefully.

## Sustainability Considerations

- Can the client add new records without breaking the structure?
- Are field labels understandable?
- Are pages and archive sections clearly named?
- Is there room for future categories or datasets?
- Are screenshots, notes, or steps available for repeat workflows?
- Can another person understand the project later?

## What This Demonstrates

- Client-centered implementation.
- Technical documentation.
- Long-term platform thinking.
- Content governance.
- Stakeholder communication and training.
